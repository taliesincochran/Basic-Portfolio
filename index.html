About Me

I grew up in rural Stanley County, N.C. on a horse farm. I learned to work hard and to never own a horse. I escaped to the North Carolina School of Math and Science for high school and N.C. State University for college, graduating with a B.S. in Biochemistry. After that I worked at N.C. State in Dominique Robertson's viral genomics laboratory and then at Catalent Pharma Solutions in stability testing for pharmaceuticals. I then became a father and stay at home dad, which was the greatest blessing of my life. Being a curious person interested in a variety of subjects, I decided it was time to learn something new, so I enrolled in the Coding Boot Camp at U.N.C. Chapel Hill and I'm learning all sorts of neat things to help me become a full stack developer.
